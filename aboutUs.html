BMW LOGO HISTORY

1917-1933

The 1917 BMW logo, the first iteration of the iconic roundel, features a circular design with a black border. 1 Inside, four quadrants alternate between blue and white, representing the Bavarian flag. 1 While often associated with a spinning propeller, the logo's primary symbolism is tied to BMW's Bavarian heritage.

1933-1953

The 1933 BMW logo was a refined version of the original design, featuring a thicker, more defined outer ring and a bolder, sharper wordmark. 1 The blue and white quadrants remained, symbolizing the company's aviation heritage, while the gold accents added a touch of luxury. This logo design marked a significant step in BMW's branding evolution, laying the foundation for the iconic logo we recognize today.

1953-1963

The 1953 BMW logo featured a more streamlined and modern design compared to its predecessors. 1 The iconic blue and white quadrants, symbolizing a rotating propeller, were retained, but the overall look was refined with softer lines and a silver color palette. 1 This logo marked a significant shift in BMW's branding, emphasizing a focus on innovation and progress

1963-1997

>

The 1963 BMW logo featured a refined design with a darker blue color and a more pronounced white line surrounding the center. 1 This logo became iconic, adorning some of BMW's most legendary vehicles like the Neue Klasse and the E30 M3. While the spinning propeller myth persists, the logo's true significance lies in its representation of the Bavarian flag, symbolizing BMW's deep-rooted connection to its Bavarian heritage.

1997-2020

>

The 1997 BMW logo introduced a more modern and three-dimensional design. 1 It retained the iconic circular shape with the blue and white quadrants, often interpreted as a spinning propeller, symbolizing BMW's aviation heritage. The new logo featured thicker black lines separating the quadrants, giving it a more defined and contemporary look. This design remained a prominent feature of BMW vehicles for several years, reflecting the brand's commitment to innovation and performance

2020-2024

BMW unveiled a simplified version of its iconic logo in 2020. The new logo retains the familiar blue and white quadrants, symbolizing the Bavarian flag and the company's origins. However, the design is cleaner and more minimalist, reflecting BMW's focus on digitalization and future mobility. This updated logo represents a modern interpretation of the brand's rich heritage, emphasizing its commitment to innovation and progress.
